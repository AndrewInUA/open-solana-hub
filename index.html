What is Solana?

Solana is public infrastructure for moving, holding, and using digital value – peer to peer, without a bank in the middle. Think of it as a shared ledger anyone can read, with rules enforced by code. That opens a layer of the internet where you can own digital things directly – money, art, tickets, identity – instead of renting access from a company.

Frequently asked questions

What is Solana in simple terms?

Solana is a public network for moving and holding digital value without a bank in the middle – a shared ledger anyone can read, with rules enforced by code. SOL is its native token, used for small fees and optional staking. Start with what Solana and SOL are.

How do I start learning Solana as a beginner?

Read the basics, then how to stay safe, then first practical steps with a wallet. Staking is an optional last step – there is no rush.

How do I assess Solana validators before staking?

Look beyond APY at commission, long-term stability, live voting status, and pool stake splits. Our guide to assessing validators explains each metric, and the Validator Transparency Dashboard shows them for every mainnet validator.
