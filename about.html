About the Hub

Open Solana Hub combines plain-English Solana education with an open validator comparison dashboard. It is written for two audiences at once: people who are new to Solana and want to understand before they act, and delegators, researchers, and operators who want transparent, comparable validator data – calm tone for both, jargon for neither.

How the two parts fit together

The learning pages explain blockchain, wallets, safety, and staking without assuming a technical background. The Compare section is the Validator Transparency Dashboard: all-time stability scores from daily full-network snapshots, commission history, voting consistency, and stake-pool splits for every mainnet validator. Education first, data when you need it – staking remains an optional last step, and the Hub never recommends a specific validator.

Who built it

I am Andrii, known in the Solana community as AndrewInUA. I operate a Solana validator and build open tools for delegators – not as a traditional software developer, but as someone who cares about transparency, clear language, and bridges between complex systems and everyday users.

Before Solana I worked in journalism and communications. I joined the network in 2022 looking for resilient, meaningful infrastructure. The Hub is one way I pay that forward: explain first, let you explore, let you decide.
